## Transitioning from Law to Computing

As a mature student at 25, I made the initial change from law to computing after a careers meeting and some personal realisations. upon reflection whle studying Being able to set aside certain morals and personal views I could not uphold, plus enjoying the creativity behind previous IT study academically and in my personal life, I decided to pursue this path. Throughout school, I was never accepted onto computer science or coding courses due to grades. As time has gone on, I have used my own personal time to try and learn independently.

As a new change in life, I wanted to take this opportunity as a fresh start, learning new skills do things differently, and have an optimistic outlook while also bringing skills forward from previous study (law) and experience that others may not be able to bring to the table. This enhances overall personal development and commits me to more self-reflection and improvement. I have recently gained a placement on a computing course, which should hopefully start the building blocks to having academic credibility in computing and devlopment, currently building a portfolio of projects to showcase understanding and knowledge in spare time.

this website also being a working projects itself, My goal with this website is not only to showcase myself as a person and to enhance networking and potential work opportunities and collaborations, but also to enhance other soft-skills and coding abilities. Ive always enjoyed a bit of writng so ffering my personal perspetive on topics Through blog posts, I aim to showcase the highs and lows of someone who does not come from the traditional background or posess the skill starting from beginner and working my way up, while not being able to access the same opportunities as others due to academic setbacks. This website and various projects serve as a reminder and living proof that in the end, it was possible.
